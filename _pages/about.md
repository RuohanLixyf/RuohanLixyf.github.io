I am a Ph.D. Candidate and Graduate Research Assistant at the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, College of Engineering, [Villanova University](https://www1.villanova.edu/university.html), PA, USA. I am the member of the [Villanova Human Mobility Data Lab (NovaMobility)](https://cfxiong.github.io/data-lab/) supervised by [Dr. Chenfeng Xiong](https://cfxiong.github.io/). 
My research centers on human mobility, large-scale location-based services (LBS) data, and the modeling, interpretation, and reconstruction of travel trajectories. I work at the intersection of data-driven mobility analytics and computational modeling, with a strong focus on map matching, trajectory structures, and the methodological foundations required to make sense of noisy, high-resolution movement data. Beyond trajectory analysis, I am also interested in the broader implications of mobility systems, including environmental impacts, urban accessibility, and demand-supply mismatches in transit systems, especially when these issues can be quantified through large-scale empirical data. Across my work, I aim to integrate machine learning, probabilistic modeling, and deep learning with principled, interpretable frameworks for understanding mobility behavior. 
My current projects include developing new map-matching algorithms for LBS data, particularly NovaMatch, a unified and extensible pipeline for matching any form of trajectory data to networks, advancing trajectory modeling theories, and evaluating mobility-based environmental and equity outcomes.
